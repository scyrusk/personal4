How can we design better user-facing cybersecurity systems?

Sauvik Das

Understand your starting point (2 mins)

  • Mobile authentication: How can we make mobile authentication more social?
  • Updating software: How can we convince users to keep their software updated?
  • Two-factor authentication: How can we make the benefits of two-factor authentication understandable?
  • Software installation: How can we make users value security in deciding which software to install?
  • Phishing: How can we make suspicious links and webpages more visible?
  • Encryption: How can we make encrypting e-mail easier?

Feel free to narrow down the problem as much as you'd like. These are just starting points.

Discuss opportunities with your group (~10 min)

What approaches can help solve your problem? Does it have to be a technology driven approach?

Who are the relevant stakeholders? End-users may not be the only actors with a vested interest in their security.

What are the shortcomings or challenges in implementing different approaches?

Remember that usability should ideally not compromise security. But, ask yourself if the assumptions of how much security is needed is appropriate in all contexts.

I might ask a representative of your group to share your thoughts with the class.
